What Are QR Codes and How Do They Work?

Quick Response (QR) codes are two-dimensional barcodes that store information readable by smartphone cameras. Unlike traditional barcodes, QR codes can hold significantly more data‚Äîincluding URLs, contact information, WiFi credentials, and payment details. When scanned using a QR code scanner or native camera app, these codes instantly direct users to digital content without manual typing.

Best Practices for QR Code Implementation

To maximize the effectiveness of your custom QR codes, follow these expert recommendations:

  • Ensure sufficient contrast between the code and background
  • Maintain a minimum size of 2x2 cm for print materials
  • Always test scan functionality before mass distribution
  • Include a call-to-action (CTA) telling users what to expect
  • Use dynamic QR codes for campaigns requiring analytics or updates

Static vs. Dynamic QR Codes: Which to Choose?

Static QR codes contain fixed information that cannot be changed after creation‚Äîideal for permanent details like WiFi passwords or contact information. Dynamic QR codes allow you to update the destination URL without reprinting, perfect for marketing campaigns where tracking and flexibility are crucial.
